About Holi

Holi is a Hindu festival that celebrates spring, love, and new life. It’s known as the "Festival of Colors" and is celebrated by throwing colored powders at each other, dancing, and enjoying festive treats.

History of Holi

The festival commemorates the victory of **good over evil** with the burning of the demoness **Holika**. The next day, people throw colored powders to celebrate the triumph of good and the arrival of spring.

Why Holi is Special

  • It marks the arrival of spring.
  • It celebrates love and forgiveness.
  • People unite, dance, and share festive sweets.
